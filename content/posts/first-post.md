## Time for a Website Update?  

I've been through stages in my affair with front end development. I learned very early on that I wasn't a designer, but I could write the code that was needed to put someone else's designs on the web. After a few years of playing with that, and pursuing it heavily, taking some freelance work during my early college years, I really got lost. 

Getting lost means, I took too much time off right as HTML5 and CCS3 blew up, and by the time I opened up a text editor again, there were frameworks I'd never heard of, new symantics that I didn't understand, and it was really overwhelming, so I walked away. (Dabbling in Python and Java since then, I've found that development not on the front end is much more my thing)

Flash forward to 2016 and my move to San Francisco from Rochester, NY. I though that taking and converting my website to a WordPress blog would be a great idea, even if no one read it, I could catalog my journey as I learned and grew, etc. Well that lasted all of 4 or 5 posts, and list got in the way. 

It's been almost a year I think since I touched that site, and I think its about time for a change. What I really need is a landing page, an online resume. 

Enter GitHub Pages. I've forked a theme that I liked, and have been tweaking content (text and images) and will be modifying fonts, colors and a few other things over the rest of this weekend, but other than that, I think it may stay pretty static.

There's a posting functionality built it (obviously, or this wouldn't be possible), so I may try and jot some thoughts into a blog post now and then, but my track record with that has been rocky at best.
