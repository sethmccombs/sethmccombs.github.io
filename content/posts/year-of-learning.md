## What's 2018 All About? 

For those that have never heard of it, I've been a big fan of the podcast [Cortex](https://www.relay.fm/cortex) for quite some time. In the most recent Eipsode (62), host's Myke Hurley and CGP Grey discuss their "yearly themes." Now these aren't a new years resolution like many people think, this isn't a "lose ten pounds in a month" type deal, these are year long themes that they live their year by. 

I've had a thought after listening to this episode, and previous year in review episodes that they've done, what is my yearly theme? I mulled this over for the last few days, unsure of where I would land on this. I needed a theme, not a resolution, resolutions fade, I wanted to do something new, or change something, or at least have something in my mind while I went through this year, what was it going to be? 

I think I've finally settled on it, looking back again at Episode 62 of Cortex, themes, as discussed, weren't something that you can force, you can possibly get 6 months into the year and still not make a decision, maybe nothing worked out, maybe the theme you chose just wasn't the right one. 

This year for me, is going to be my year of learning. I capped off 2017 by making the biggest career move I ever have (arguably bigger than moving to San Francisco), and has spent my first month getting acquainted with my new life in a start-up, and am ready to get down to business.  

Learning for me is a broad term, I've spent the last 2 years reading roughly 30 books a year, I'm going to keep that up, while focusing on no subject or genre in particular. I'm enrolled in multiple online tutorial sites and have been for some time. What I've come to realize, is that I've been too many places at once. While I've spent time prior years learning, this time, I'm going to focus on what I'm learning, more of the same things, less of a little of everything. 2018 is the year of __focused__ learning.

I've started making a short list of things that I aim to learn, re-learn, or expand my knowledge on this year, both professionally and personally, and also a little justification why I'll be focusing on it. I think this list might be organic, and it may change this year, but I'd like to only add things, never remove. 

1. Java - It's everywhere, love it, hate it, it's something I've worked in before, it has it's uses at my current employer, and I have a drive-by familiarity with, that I will be expanding on this year. (Also, as Java knowledge grows, will be folding some Kotlin in there too)

2. Python - Python was the first language I ever learned to program, and would like to get back to where I was.

3. Guitar - I used to play upwards of 3-4 hours a day, minimum, now I'm lucky to play 3-4 times a month. I'll be focusing on at least 10 minutes a day, structured, unstructured, noodling, whatever, I need to get back to music. 

4. Cloud Infrasctructure - Since I've been working at SourceClear, I've been diving into a world of Docker, Kubernetes and other cloud stuff. It's stuff I have experience with, but purely in an academic sense, but lacking in the practical experience. 

It's a short list, and I may come across a few things to add, but the whole point of this is a small list, a few things to learn well, as opposed to a long list of things to learn a few things about. The goal is to build expertise in a few things. Once I've got some of this stuff nailed down, I can branch out. 

We'll see how this goes. Goals are 15-20 minutes a day, probably splitting the Python and Java stuff, I'm not keen on learning two programming languages at the same time. Maybe I'll re-write Java programs I write in Python, I don't know. This is all an experiment, but I feel like I'm on the right track already.
