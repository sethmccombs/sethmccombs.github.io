## Where Have I Been? 

So, I'll start off by being honest. I forgot this site existed. For anyone that is a friend and doesn't see me day to day, that wants to follow my escapades can do so on Twitter and Instagram. Twitter for rants and such, Instagram for videos of my guitar playing, and photos of my cat. Pretty par for the course.

I've said it before, and I'll say it again, I think I'm going to keep this blog up to date, trying to write once or twice a month. Ideally, I'd have something to post here once a week, that's the stretch goal, so we'll see. I haven't posted here since January, so I have a lot of catch up to do!

#### So lets bring the world up to speed, what's happened in the last 10 months?

The first week in December, I started my new role at SourceClear. I was hired as IT Operations engineer, first tasked with cleaning up the IT side of the SourceClear world, on/offboarding, MDM, processes, and everything else. I spent my first few months learning about what SourceClear does, and a basic understanding of our Kubernetes cluster based infrastructure.

RSA 2018 roles around...and the announcement drops. We've been acquired by CA Technologies to fold into their year old acquisition, Veracode. What does this mean? Everything I've worked on for my IT plan, new software and Saas, etc, out the window. CA and Veracode have IT, where does this leave me? I'm handed an offer letter for "Principal" IT, here's where the issue lies, I was hired to transition to a DevOps role, so this is definitely not something I'm keen on. I tell those in charge "Thanks but no thanks, I'll see where else I can go"

At this point I'm introducted to the manager of the SRE/DevOps team at VeraCode, and asked if I want a spot on the team. Really? That's _exactly_ what I want. Fast forward one month in, it's June 2018, and I'm handed one of the largest internal projects at all of Veracode, our GitLab based SCM platform, and that automation that builds it. Jump forward to August, and my mentor at Sourceclear, the first engineering mentor I've ever had has decided to move on, and I am inheriting the care and feeding of our Kubernetes clusters.

Looking back on the summer, I'm now in the best position I've ever been careerwise. I love my defacto position as the containerization and kubernetes evangelist in the company. Spreading the good work of Kubernetes, and teaching anyone that'll listen.
